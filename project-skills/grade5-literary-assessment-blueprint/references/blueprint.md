# Five-item chapter blueprint

## Fixed slots

| Slot | Primary target | Standard | Typical DOK | Required item action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | Explicit comprehension | RL.5.1 | 1 | Locate an important stated detail, action, setting fact, or reason |
| Q2 | Character/event relationship | RL.5.3 | 2 | Explain motivation, response, cause/result, or interaction |
| Q3 | Vocabulary/phrase in context | RL.5.4 or L.5.4/5.5 | 1-2 | Infer meaning/effect using usable local context |
| Q4 | Evidence-based inference | RL.5.1 | 2 | Infer a trait, feeling, problem, or likely conclusion from at least two clues |
| Q5 | Synthesis rotation | RL.5.2, RL.5.5, or RL.5.6 | 2-3 | Summarize/theme, explain structural role, or analyze point of view |

## Q5 rotation for a ten-chapter book

| Chapters | Q5 target |
|---|---|
| 1, 4, 7, 10 | Summary (RL.5.2) |
| 2, 5, 8 | Theme or central message (RL.5.2) |
| 3, 6 | Structural role of an event/chapter (RL.5.5) |
| 9 | Point of view and its effect (RL.5.6) |

## Book-level distribution (50 items)

- 10 explicit comprehension
- 10 character/event relationship
- 10 vocabulary/phrase in context
- 10 evidence-based inference
- 7 summary/theme
- 2 structure
- 1 point of view

## Demand and accessibility

- About 20 DOK 1, 25 DOK 2, and 5 DOK 3 items. A difficult word does not raise DOK.
- Use a chapter's beginning, middle, and end across the five evidence sets.
- Favor consequential details over trivia such as unimportant colors, numbers, or names.
- Use selected-response items only when one option is demonstrably best.
