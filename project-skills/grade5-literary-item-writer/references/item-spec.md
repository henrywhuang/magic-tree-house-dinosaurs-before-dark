# Item specification

## General form

- Four options labeled A-D; exactly one best answer.
- Use natural U.S. English appropriate for strong Grade 5 multilingual learners.
- Keep stems direct, normally 8-18 words excluding quotations.
- Keep options parallel in grammar, content type, and approximate length.
- Avoid `all of the above`, `none of the above`, absolutes such as `always/never` unless the text supports them, and clues based on grammar or option length.

## By item type

### Q1 explicit comprehension

Ask about an important fact, action, setting detail, or explicitly stated reason. Distractors should be locally plausible but false; do not paste events from other chapters.

### Q2 relationship

Ask why a character acts, how one event affects another, or how two characters/events relate. The correct answer must express the relationship, not merely repeat one event.

### Q3 vocabulary/phrase

Quote the sentence containing the word or phrase. Ask for meaning or effect in that context. The nearby sentence must provide a clue. Options should share the same part of speech and not be synonyms of one another.

### Q4 inference

Ask what the reader can infer about a trait, feeling, problem, or conclusion. Require at least two compatible details. The rationale must name both clues even if the stem quotes only one.

### Q5 synthesis

- Summary: include only central events in correct causal/chronological order; distractors omit a key event, add a minor detail, or distort the outcome.
- Theme: state a transferable message, not a one-word topic or a plot sentence; support it with character response and outcome.
- Structure: ask how an event launches, develops, complicates, or resolves the chapter's action.
- Point of view: ask how third-person narration focused through a character shapes what the reader knows or feels.

## Answer-position check

Across ten chapters target 11-14 answers per letter. Within a chapter do not repeat one letter more than twice.

## Evidence check

- Preserve exact source wording except for correcting obvious OCR punctuation.
- Evidence must come from the assigned chapter.
- Rationale should state why the correct option fits; it should not merely say `because the text says so`.
