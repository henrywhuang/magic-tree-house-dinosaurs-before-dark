# Grade 5 Multilingual Reading Standards

Read [references/standards-map.md](references/standards-map.md) before selecting targets.

## Workflow

1. Treat the learner as a strong Grade 5 multilingual reader: preserve grade-level thinking while removing avoidable linguistic clutter from directions and options.
2. Select only standards supported by the supplied literary text. Prioritize RL.5.1-RL.5.6 and L.5.4-L.5.5.
3. Require every assessed claim to be answerable from the assigned chapter. Do not rely on background knowledge, another chapter, film adaptations, or Chinese translation.
4. Keep source wording when it carries evidence; simplify only the question language.
5. Return a standard code, a short skill statement, and the observable student action for each target.

## Non-negotiables

- Do not lower an inference task into fact recall merely because learners are non-native speakers.
- Do not test rare vocabulary in the stem unless the word itself is the target.
- Do not label simple chronology as theme, inference, or structure.
- Do not claim RL.5.2 unless the student identifies theme or summarizes essential events without minor details.
- Do not claim RL.5.4/L.5.4 unless context in the quoted sentence or nearby text supports the meaning.
